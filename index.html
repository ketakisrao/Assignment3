1G: Overlay Chart

Design Choices for the above chart:

  • The chart above groups the four causes of deaths: 1) Heart Disease, 2) Cancer, 3) Drug Use, and 4) Motor Vehicle Accident.
  • Each class is represented with its own color, denoted by the legend to the right, to make it easier for the user to map a particular color to a cause of death and quickly compare it with the adjacent bars without having to look at a label below or to the sides.
  • The bars are arranged in increasing order of age (bins). There are labels present just in case the user finds it hard to distiguish between two colors(maybe they're color blind).
  • These bars are grouped together to represent one age bin and there is enough space between two groups for the user to be able to distinguish between two adjacent groups.

2B: Successful Visualization from 2A

According to me, the most successful visualization from part 2A is the one for Miles. It perfectly shows the confidence levels of the cause of death prediction algorithm. The user (Miles) has a fair idea of his likely cause of death but also knows that the algorithm is only about 30% confident about it. Visualizing this uncertainity would help the algorithm gain the user's trust.

2C: Good & Bad questions for 2A

Good Questions:

  • Given my age, gender, education, ethnicity and marital status, what is my likely cause of death?
  • What are the sub categories under my likely cause of death?
  • What is the confidence with which this application can predict my likely cause of death?
  • What did the algorithm base it's predictions on?

Bad Questions:

  • Why did the algorithm predict what it predicted?
  • Why did it chose one cause of death over another?
  • Why did it not chose a particular cause of death?

2D: Interactive Cause of death prediction

*Note: this application might not be inclusive of all ethnicities, ages, genders, etc or even causes of death. It bases it's predictions off of the data given by CDC (Center for Disease Control and Prevention) which is a federal public health and safety agency of the United States.

Deaths Prediction

Above is an interactive version of the data that can be input into this application which predicts the likely cause of death for the user. The application asks for demographic data such as Age, Gender, Education and Race, and personal data such as Marital Status.

  • Age: As observed, from the charts in part 1, age influences the cause of death to a great extent which makes it an important factor to include for prediction.
  • Gender: Another factor which influences this prediction algorithm is the gender of that person, and therefore is a necessary input.
  • Education & Marital Status: These were two hidden factors which don't immediately come to mind when one is predicting their likely cause of death but the algorithm performs a lot better if it knows these two categories as well.
  • Axis Labels: Note that the labels of the chart clearly denote what is represented by each one of the axes.
  • Fineprint: There is a note on the top left corner of this application which states that this application might not be fully inclusive of all types of genders, ethnicities etc..
  • Demographic Data: Demographic data, like Ethnicity, might be a bit sensitive to ask for and there is a possibility that it could be taken from services like google analytics, but there is a trade off of trust between the user and the algorithm here.

2E: Bin-ing of data

  • Age: could be binned into Children(age < 18), Adults(age < 60) and Senior Citizens(age > 60) to be able to better observe the trends in causes of death.
  • Sex: can't be binned anymore since it's a small category. I would definitelt add more options to make the application more inclusive.
  • Race: could be grouped by continents/broader race groups like American Indian, Asian, African American etc, and Asian could have several sub-categories like Chinese, Indian, Korean. This kind of indexing would make it a lot easier to search for a particular ethnicity
  • Education: This field is already binned and cannot be binned further.
  • Month_of_death: This field could be binned on the basis of seasons since the each season has distinct weather that could impact the cause of death.
  • Day_of_week_of_death: Could be binned into weekday or weekend since most people work on weekdays and be at home on weekends which could corelate with accidental/non-accidental causes of death.
  • Relationship_status: This field cannot be binned further.
  • Cause: Could be binned into similar causes or similar group of disease.
  • Group: Groups like 'Asthma', 'Respiratory Condition' and 'Issues breathing' could be binned into one common group 'Breathing Issues' (which is also easier to understand) for better understanding of the cause of death for users.
