1G: Overlay Chart

Design Choices for the above chart:

  • The chart above groups the four causes of deaths: 1) Heart Disease, 2) Cancer, 3) Drug Use, and 4) Motor Vehicle Accident.
  • Each class is represented with its own color, denoted by the legend to the right, to make it easier for the user to map a particular color to a cause of death and quickly compare it with the adjacent bars without having to look at a label below or to the sides.
  • The bars are arranged in increasing order of age (bins). There are labels present just in case the user finds it hard to distiguish between two colors(maybe they're color blind).
  • These bars are grouped together to represent one age bin and there is enough space between two groups for the user to be able to distinguish between two adjacent groups.

2E: Bin-ing of data

  • Age: could be binned into Children(age < 18), Adults(age < 60) and Senior Citizens(age > 60) to be able to better observe the trends in causes of death.
  • Sex: can't be binned anymore since it's a small category. I would definitelt add more options to make the application more inclusive.
  • Race: could be grouped by continents/broader race groups like American Indian, Asian, African American etc, and Asian could have several sub-categories like Chinese, Indian, Korean. This kind of indexing would make it a lot easier to search for a particular ethnicity
  • Education: This field is already binned and cannot be binned further.
  • Month_of_death: This field could be binned on the basis of seasons since the each season has distinct weather that could impact the cause of death.
  • Day_of_week_of_death: Could be binned into weekday or weekend since most people work on weekdays and be at home on weekends which could corelate with accidental/non-accidental causes of death.
  • Relationship_status: This field cannot be binned further.
  • Cause: Could be binned into similar causes or similar group of disease.
  • Group: Groups like 'Asthma', 'Respiratory Condition' and 'Issues breathing' could be binned into one common group 'Breathing Issues' (which is also easier to understand) for better understanding of the cause of death for users.
